HISTORY OF NITSA

The Nigeria Immigration Training School Ahoada (NITSA)

is the Immigration Assistant Cadre training body of the Nigerian Immigration Service and is charged with the responsibility of training potential officers and men of the Service.

 

As a part of the effort to raise the quality of service delivery in Nigeria Immigration service, the idea of establishing additional training school arose. This led to the establishment NITSA and command staff sokoto , Nigeria immigration training school orlu under the leadership of the then CGIS.

NITSA was sited in its present location through the intense and sustained effort of late DCG Esseine and the paramount ruler of okpyer Robinson o Robinson. prior to its commencement the premises of the school was a rehabilitation centre. However, following its acquisition by the service, the school was established aand commissioned on the 20th september 2001 by the then Minister of Internal Affairs, Chief Sunday Afolabi

The School Kicked started with 8 students and Mr. J.A Etim, CIS (Retired) as the pioneer Commandant. It is instructuve to note that, the school has made tremendious progress both in physical infrastructure and the number of students being trained. For instance, the school has advanced from training 8 students in 2001 to 684 in 2017(20th pre basic course) with Mrs Hornby A D as the commandant in total, about 8528 students have been trained in the school with this current set inclusive.
